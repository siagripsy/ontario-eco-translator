Ontario Electricity Billing Knowledge Base

----------------------------------------
PRICING SYSTEM OVERVIEW
----------------------------------------

There are three main electricity pricing plans in Ontario:

1. Time-of-Use (TOU)
2. Ultra-Low Overnight (ULO)
3. Tiered Pricing

----------------------------------------
TIME-OF-USE (TOU)
----------------------------------------

Electricity price depends on time of day.

There are three periods:

- Off-Peak:
  Lowest demand periods
  Typically nights and weekends

- Mid-Peak:
  Moderate demand periods
  Typically daytime but not busiest hours

- On-Peak:
  Highest demand periods
  Typically when people are active at home

Seasonal schedule:

SUMMER (May 1 – October 31):
- On-Peak: 11 AM – 5 PM
- Mid-Peak: 7–11 AM and 5–7 PM
- Off-Peak: 7 PM – 7 AM + weekends

WINTER (November 1 – April 30):
- On-Peak: 7–11 AM and 5–7 PM
- Mid-Peak: 11 AM – 5 PM
- Off-Peak: 7 PM – 7 AM + weekends

Note: weekends and statutory holidays are always off-peak.

----------------------------------------
ULTRA-LOW OVERNIGHT (ULO)
----------------------------------------

Four pricing periods:

- Ultra-Low Overnight: 
  Overnight hours (lowest cost)(11:00 PM - 7:00 AM)

- Weekend Off-Peak:
  Entire weekends and holidays (7:00 AM - 11:00 PM)

- Mid-Peak:
  Daytime moderate usage ( 7:00 AM - 4:00 PM + 9:00 PM - 11:00 PM)

- On-Peak:
  Highest demand periods (4:00 PM - 9:00 PM )

ULO schedule is consistent year-round.

----------------------------------------
TIERED PRICING
----------------------------------------

Electricity price depends on total usage, not time.

Residential thresholds:

- Summer (May–Oct):
  Tier 1: up to 600 kWh
  Tier 2: above 600 kWh

- Winter (Nov–Apr):
  Tier 1: up to 1000 kWh
  Tier 2: above 1000 kWh

----------------------------------------
APPROXIMATE PRICES (FOR ANALYSIS)
----------------------------------------

These are typical Ontario residential rates:

TOU:
- Off-Peak: ~8.7 cents per kWh
- Mid-Peak: ~12.2 cents per kWh
- On-Peak: ~18.2 cents per kWh

ULO:
- Ultra-Low Overnight: ~2.4 cents per kWh
- Weekend Off-Peak: ~8.7 cents per kWh
- Mid-Peak: ~12.2 cents per kWh
- On-Peak: ~28.6 cents per kWh

Tiered:
- Tier 1: ~10.3 cents per kWh
- Tier 2: ~12.5 cents per kWh

----------------------------------------
COMMON BILL COMPONENTS
----------------------------------------

1. Electricity Charge:
   Based on kWh and pricing plan

2. Delivery Charge:
   Covers infrastructure and distribution
   Often a large portion of the bill

3. Regulatory Charge:
   Administrative costs

4. HST:
   13% tax applied to total

----------------------------------------
IMPORTANT INSIGHTS FOR ANALYSIS
----------------------------------------

- High bills are often caused by delivery charges, not usage
- Using electricity during On-Peak increases cost significantly
- Tiered pricing is better for consistent usage patterns
- ULO is best for users with high overnight consumption (e.g., EV charging)
